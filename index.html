🏇 How the AI Sees the Race Unfolding

The Start: A clean break for 34 runners. Haiti Couleurs (Sean Bowen) blazes into the lead as expected, with Final Orders (Conor Stone-Walsh) and Spanish Harlem (Brian Hayes) also prominent. Johnnywho (R P McLernon) drops in mid-division, tucked on the rail. I Am Maximus (Paul Townend) and Grangeclare West (Patrick Mullins) settle behind the pace, saving energy. Iroko (Jonjo O'Neill Jr) is positioned handier than last year, tracking the leaders in sixth or seventh.

First Circuit: The field streams over Becher's Brook and The Canal Turn. Jagwar (Mark Walsh), seeing these unique fences for the first time, jumps big and wastes energy. Captain Cody, who fell earlier in the season, makes a mistake at Valentine's. Several novices depart. By the second circuit, the 34 are thinning. Haiti Couleurs' lead starts to shrink; his high weight of 11-10 tells. Johnnywho is travelling ominously well in behind, McLernon sitting still while others are already being pushed along.

The Business End: At the fourth-last, the race begins in earnest. Iroko moves smoothly into contention — but I Am Maximus, under 11-12, is already under pressure from Townend. Grangeclare West jumps superbly under Patrick Mullins and pings the third-last. Johnnywho sweeps wide on the outside, a relentless engine picking off tiring horses. Gorgeous Tom (Sean Flanagan) emerges from the pack at 20/1, travelling as well as anything.

The Run-In: Johnnywho hits the famous Elbow in front — 6lb well-in and fresh from the Ultima, he's peaking at exactly the right moment. Iroko closes gamely but can't match the winner's finishing kick. Grangeclare West runs a huge race for third, vindicating Patrick Mullins' decision to ride him. I Am Maximus is heroic in fourth — one-two-four in three successive Nationals is extraordinary but the weight just finds him out. Stellar Story runs on powerfully for R T Dunne into fifth, the 21lb pull doing its work. Gorgeous Tom completes the frame in sixth, justifying every expert tip.
